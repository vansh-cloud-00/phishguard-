Learn Phishing

Master how phishing works, its types, and how to spot attacks before they catch you.

🎣

THE SIMPLE TRUTH

Phishing is manipulation disguised as trust. Attackers study human psychology — our fear, urgency, curiosity, and greed — to make us act without thinking.

The word "phishing" comes from "fishing" — casting fake bait and waiting for a victim to bite. And just like fishing, the bait is designed to look completely real.

🧠

THE PSYCHOLOGY

Attackers use these triggers to bypass your logical thinking:

  • Fear – "Your account will be closed!"
  • Urgency – "Act within 24 hours!"
  • Authority – "This is your bank speaking"
  • Curiosity – "You've won a prize!"
  • Greed – "Click here to claim ₹10,000"

EMAIL PHISHING

The oldest and most common type. Attackers send millions of fake emails that mimic banks, tech companies, or government agencies. These emails contain urgent messages and malicious links.

⚠ Watch for: Suspicious sender domain, spelling errors, urgent tone, links that don't match the company's real URL.
🌐

FAKE WEBSITES (Clone Sites)

Attackers create pixel-perfect copies of real websites. The URL looks slightly different (e.g., "paypa1.com" instead of "paypal.com"). Your login credentials are stolen instantly.

⚠ Watch for: Check the full URL before entering any password. Look for HTTPS. Even HTTPS can be fake — always verify the domain name.
📱

SMISHING (SMS Phishing)

Fake SMS messages pretend to be from your bank or delivery service. "Your parcel is held. Pay ₹50 to release it: [link]" — The link leads to a fake payment page.

⚠ Watch for: Unexpected SMS from unknown numbers, requests to click a link or share an OTP, urgency about account or delivery issues.
📞

VISHING (Voice Phishing)

Fake phone calls from "bank agents" or "CBI officers" who pressure you into sharing OTPs, account details, or making emergency transfers. Often uses spoofed numbers.

⚠ Watch for: No real bank/government ever asks for your OTP over phone. Hang up and call the official number directly.
🎯

SPEAR PHISHING

Highly targeted attacks using your personal information. The attacker researches you on LinkedIn or social media and crafts a personalized email that feels very legitimate.

⚠ Watch for: Emails that use your real name, reference your company, or mention a colleague. Even highly personalized emails can be fake.
🐋

WHALING (CEO FRAUD)

Spear phishing that specifically targets executives (CEOs, CFOs). Attackers impersonate the CEO to instruct finance teams to transfer money "urgently."

⚠ Watch for: Unusual payment requests via email, no phone confirmation. Always verify large transactions via a second channel (call the person directly).

Real vs Fake — Learn to Spot the Difference

// URL COMPARISON

✅ LEGITIMATE URLS
  • Uses the exact official domain
  • Has valid HTTPS certificate
  • No extra words or hyphens
  • No numbers replacing letters
✓ https://www.sbi.co.in/login
✓ https://accounts.google.com
✓ https://www.amazon.in
🚨 PHISHING URLS
  • Misspelled or extra words in domain
  • Numbers replacing letters (e.g., "0" for "o")
  • Strange TLDs (.tk, .info, .xyz)
  • Subdomains mimicking real sites
✗ https://sbi-verify-account.tk/login
✗ https://g00gle.account-secure.com
✗ https://amazon-india-offer.xyz

// EMAIL COMPARISON

✅ REAL EMAIL TRAITS
  • Comes from official domain (@sbi.co.in)
  • Greets you by your full name
  • No urgent threats or pressure
  • Links go to official domain
  • Professionally written, no typos
🚨 PHISHING EMAIL TRAITS
  • From generic or spoofed address
  • Generic greeting ("Dear Customer")
  • Creates panic and urgency
  • Links to suspicious third-party sites
  • Spelling mistakes, bad grammar

🛡 YOUR DEFENSE CHECKLIST

  1. Always check the URL before entering your password
  2. Never share OTPs with anyone — not even your bank
  3. Enable 2-Factor Authentication on all accounts
  4. Don't click links in emails — type the URL manually
  5. Verify unexpected requests via phone call
  6. Use a password manager to detect fake sites
  7. Keep your browser and OS updated
  8. Report phishing emails to your email provider

🔑 THE GOLDEN RULE

If something creates urgency, fear, or seems too good to be true — STOP. Take a breath. Verify through official channels before doing anything.

💡 Think before you click. Attackers rely on your gut reaction. Your pause is your protection.

✅ SAFE vs SUSPICIOUS

🟢Safe: Official email, your name used, link goes to real domain
🟡Suspicious: Unexpected message, unknown sender, urgency used
🔴Danger: Asks for OTP/password, weird URL, generic greeting, threats
